The Strategic Mandate

Why a Second, More Focused Workshop?

While the initial convening in Singapore mapped out the generalized theoretical frameworks of US-Singapore R-CPIS cooperation, the D.C. follow-up demands actionable specificity. We converge at a historical juncture governed by two accelerating trends:

💡
Roadmap Objective: The sole deliverable of this invite-only session is the compilation of a high-impact research prospectus directing collaborative cross-border research funding on infrastructure survivability.
ACCELERATION 01

Disruptive Technologies

Rapid convergence of quantum engineering, decentralized autonomous robotics, and agentic AI. These offer breakthrough protective capabilities while simultaneously presenting unprecedented system vulnerability surfaces.

  • ✓ Quantum optimization algorithms
  • ✓ Autonomous physical repairs
  • ✓ Generative cyber-security barriers
ACCELERATION 02

Compounding Hazards

Increasing frequency, severity, and cascading correlation of natural disasters (extreme climate events) and artificial, coordinated threat vectors (state-sponsored cyber assaults, complex physical sabotage).

  • ⚠ Concurrent black-sky incidents
  • ⚠ Trans-boundary dependencies
  • ⚠ Fast-developing system failures
Scientific Domains

Focus Areas & Technical Pillars

Rather than a broad disciplinary survey, the D.C. convening focuses heavily on three critical, cutting-edge intersections of security and engineering.

1

Quantum Engineering for Infrastructure Systems

Leveraging quantum sensors for preemptive material decay detection and quantum computing paradigms to simulate extremely complex, high-dimensional failure dynamics.

2

Resilience Red Teaming

Constructing systematic adversarial framework models designed to aggressively exploit hidden inter-dependencies, validating digital twins, and assessing dynamic failover configurations.

3

Unconventional & Nature-Based Solutions

Blending ecological infrastructure adaptations (nature-based barriers, bio-mimetic structures) with cyber-physical telemetry to build resilient hybrid protective mechanisms.

Pillar 1 Scope US-SG R-CPIS Alliance

Quantum Engineering

Quantum sensors will allow us to measure real-time micro-stresses on bridges, underground water pipelines, and power substations at precision rates previously unimaginable. Concurrently, developing scalable algorithms on upcoming quantum computers prepares our grid operations to dynamic optimization protocols even during total regional signal failures.

Key Roadmap Chapter Questions:
  • • How can quantum key distribution (QKD) be scaled cheaply to secure operational technology networks?
  • • What are the constraints of current-generation NISQ computers for complex load-balancing simulations?
